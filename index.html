Building from the Ground Up

My drive to build and lead took root early on. While earning my Master's in Political Science at Fordham University and achieving recognition as one of New York's 30 Under 30 political operatives, I knew my real calling was not just shaping policy from a distance. It was doing the heavy lifting on the ground.

I stepped into foundational care roles running a three-person personal care home and serving as the Executive Director of a childcare center. Those roles taught me firsthand what it means to support families when they are at their most vulnerable.

Scaling Impact in Philadelphia

Eventually, that grassroots perspective scaled. I founded a home healthcare company that grew to employ over 1,000 people in the Philadelphia region, providing vital care to more than 600 individuals.

Building and managing a healthcare organization of that magnitude requires immense grit. It was also during this operational expansion that my passion for guiding others truly took off. As the son of an educator, I began mentoring fellow leaders and entrepreneurs, starting a lifelong habit of passing down knowledge that continues to this day.

The Catalyst: Turning Failure Into Traction

I learned early on that the most profound growth does not come from the easy wins; it comes from the moments that force you to recalibrate. While I was actively building my real estate portfolio, another one of my businesses failed measurably.

It was a heavy blow. But rather than breaking me, it became the crucible for everything that followed. It forced me to strip away the noise, redefine my goals, and shift my perspective entirely toward creating tangible, lasting community impact.

Rebuilding with Purpose

That redefined focus led me to actively build communities. Following my business failure, I launched a nonprofit that guided 74 families into their very first homes. I rolled up my sleeves to rehab 11 properties in disadvantaged neighborhoods, focusing on restoring neighborhood pride alongside the brick and mortar.

The operational clarity gained from overcoming failure sharpened my instincts as an executive and asset evaluator. That ultimately paved the way for two successful corporate exits as a founding investor, and allowed me to give back by donating over $1.5 million to local charities, community development projects, and public works initiatives.

Innovating for Texans: Property Tax Relief

After relocating to Texas, my commitment to providing direct economic relief to the local community only deepened. Leveraging my background in real estate and strategy, I developed a proprietary program that has successfully lowered the property tax bills for hundreds of Texans. By demystifying the tax system, this initiative has directly kept more money in the pockets of hardworking families across the state.

The Educator's Legacy: Mentorship

My most fulfilling work happens behind the scenes. Building on the mentorship habits I started during my healthcare agency days, I currently serve as a mentor to over two dozen entrepreneurs and business leaders. Through strategic guidance and operational coaching, I have helped several founders successfully scale their operations into multimillion dollar revenue companies.

Equally rewarding is guiding individuals through foundational life milestones by coaching them step by step as they purchase their first homes or acquire commercial properties. Passing down knowledge, empowering leaders, and watching others build their own lasting legacies is without a doubt my absolute favorite thing to do.

The Anchor: Strategy & Leadership Today

Today, as the President of Catan Strategy Group, the same quiet endurance that pushes me to cross the finish line of a 3:55:43 marathon fuels my professional and investment life out of the Cypress, Texas and Greater Houston area.

But the true foundation of everything I do is not found in an investment portfolio or a corporate boardroom. It is at home. Being a husband to Rechanda and a father to our two young sons is my ultimate anchor. Every business built, every neighborhood improved, and every mile run is ultimately about leaving a legacy they can be proud of.
